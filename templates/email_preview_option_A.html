AI Workforce Transformation

McKinsey: Two-thirds of legal tasks amenable to GenAI augmentation [1]

15 March 2026 — McKinsey's Global Institute released findings showing 65% of legal work tasks can be augmented by generative AI in the next two years, with document review and contract analysis leading adoption. [1]

Opinion Takeaway: The 65% figure will be used by every vendor in their pitch decks for the next 12 months. Legal leaders need a framework for evaluating which two-thirds actually applies to their team — not all task types carry equal risk or equal ROI.

Relevance to BetterWiser: Directly supports the firm's pilot-to-production advisory work and provides a credible data anchor for change management conversations.

Anna Lozynski: "AI confidence without AI competence is the real risk" [2]

20 March 2026 — In her Substack post, Anna Lozynski argues that the gap between AI enthusiasm and AI literacy inside legal teams is wider than most GCs acknowledge, and that rushing to deploy without upskilling creates compounded governance risk. [2]

Opinion Takeaway: The framing of "confidence without competence" is immediately usable in client workshops. It names the tension without blaming individuals, making it a politically safe way to open a capability gap conversation.

Relevance to BetterWiser: Aligns directly with BetterWiser's change management positioning. This framing is worth adopting as a client-facing diagnostic tool.
